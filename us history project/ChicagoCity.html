The City of Chicago is one of the youngest cities
in the United States, as it was incorporated only in 1833 after
the end of the last Native American resistance in the surrounding
area. 15 years later, this small town got its first telegraph and
railroad systems. The famous innovations of grain elevators
and the Board of Trade's wheat grading standards allowed Chicago
to become the world's largest grain port by the year 1854.
Although for the last few years this city became known for a
number of local workers' strikes, the World's Fair is a great
opportunity for Chicago to attract new ideas and people
and demonstrate its huge potential!

Activities in Chicago

Places to Go.

Museum of
Natural History

The Museum of Natural History was established three
years ago, and was a result of support by Marshall Field,
a local business magnate that was ready to fund any plan
for increasing Chicago's access to cultural and educational
facilities. Some of the collections include the exposition
of paleontological and archaeological materials, various
 Asian plants, fungi, lichens, bryophytes, and mosses.
This museum opens a door to an astonishing world of nature
and wildlife!

The Art Institute
of Chicago

Founded in 1879, the Art Institute of
Chicago has thousands of works of art in its vast
collection. More than 30 special exhibitions are held
here annually, and some of them include paintings by
famous artists, such as Georges Seurat and Mary Cassatt.
Feel free to enter this vivid world of color and
exceptional ideas!

Lincoln Park

Formerly known as Lake Park, Lincoln Park
has been used as a city cemetery during the Civil War.
After the assassination of our 16th President, Abraham
Lincoln, the park changed its name to the current one.
Although Lincoln Park does have such a dark background,
do not hesitate to visit it today, as its lovely parklands
and golden beaches will not leave you unsatisfied!

Restaurants

Daley's Restaurant

If you begin to miss the homemade food during
the Chicago World's Fair, the Daley's Restaurant will be
a perfect option for you and your family! The menu of
Daley's Restaurant includes waffles and pork chops,
which are doubtless parts of a homestyle classics
that we are all familiar with, even today.

Schaller's Pump

After a day that was full of new experiences and
discoveries, it is perfectly normal to have a wish to relax at
the local bar with a glass of beer while chatting with your
friends about everything. In that case, you ought to
visit the Schaller's Pump, which is always ready to serve you
and your folks some whiskey and beer! They also have several
meal options, so it is exactly the place you need at the
end of the day!
